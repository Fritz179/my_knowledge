\overbrace{m(x-u) + v}^\text{point}$

From $m$ and $P(x, y)$ one can get $q = y-mx$
X-intersect: $x = q$
Y-intersect: $y = -\dfrac{q}{m}$
Preserve vector addition: $f(a + b) = f(a) + f(b)$
Preserve scalar multiplication: $a\cdot f(b) = f(a\cdot b)$
